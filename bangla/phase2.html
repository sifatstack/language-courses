✅ Week 4: Present Tense Verbs ▶

4.1 -- How Bangla Verbs Work

Unlike Arabic, Bangla verbs don't use a root-and-pattern system. Instead, verbs have a stem and you add endings based on who is doing the action and the formality level.

Key difference from Arabic: Arabic changes based on gender (بتروح / بتروحي). Bangla changes based on social register (তুই / তুমি / আপনি).
No gender! There is NO gender distinction in Bangla verbs or pronouns! সে (she) means both "he" and "she." Coming from Arabic, this will feel strange at first -- but it simplifies things enormously.

Question 1 of 3
What determines verb conjugation in Bangla?
Bangla verbs change based on social register (intimacy/formality level), not gender like Arabic.
Question 2 of 3
Does Bangla have grammatical gender?
Bangla has NO grammatical gender. সে (she) means both "he" and "she." No more worrying about masculine/feminine!
Question 3 of 3
How do Bangla verbs differ from Arabic verbs structurally?
Bangla uses stem + endings, while Arabic uses the famous root-and-pattern (جذر) system.

4.2 -- Present Simple (Habitual)

Let's conjugate করা (kora) / to do in the present simple:

PersonBanglaPronunciationMeaning
আমি (I)করিkoriI do
তুই (intimate)করিসkorisyou do
তুমি (informal)করোkoroyou do
সে / ও (he/she)করেkorehe/she does

আমরা (we)করিkoriwe do

আমি / আমরা → -ই (-i)

তুই → -ইস (-is)

আপনি → -এন (-en)

4.3 -- Present Continuous ("Right now")

To say something is happening RIGHT NOW, Bangla uses the -ছ- (-techh-) form:

আমি খাইতেছি (Ami khaitechhi) -- I am eating right now

সে কাজ করতেছে (She kaj kortechhe) -- He/She is working right now

তুমি কী করতেছো? (Tumi ki kortechho?) -- What are you doing?

আমি বাংলা শিখতেছি (Ami Bangla shikhtechhi) -- I'm learning Bangla

Syrian parallel: This works like عم + verb in Syrian. عم باكل = আমি খাইতেছি. The difference is Bangla builds it into the verb ending (-তেছ-) rather than using a separate word.

Question 1 of 3
What marker indicates present continuous in Bangla?
-তেছ- (-techh-) is the present continuous marker. -ব- is future. -ল- is past.
Question 2 of 3
How does Bangla form present continuous differently from Syrian Arabic?
Bangla builds continuous into the verb (-তেছ-), while Syrian Arabic uses a separate word عم before the verb.
Question 3 of 3
How do you say "What are you doing?" (informal) in Bangla?
তুমি কী করতেছো? (Tumi ki kortechho?) uses the continuous form -তেছ- to ask about right now.

✅ Week 5: Past Tense ▶

5.1 -- Simple Past ("I did")

Bangla past tense uses the -ল- (-l-) marker:

PersonBanglaPronunciationMeaning
আমি (I)করলামkorlamI did
তুই (intimate)করলিkorliyou did
তুমি (informal)করলাkorlayou did
সে (he/she)করলোkorlohe/she did
আপনি (formal)করলেনkorlenyou did
আমরা (we)করলামkorlamwe did
তারা (they)করলোkorlothey did

Negation -- "I didn't":

আমি যাই নাই (Ami jai nai) -- I didn't go

আমি দেখি নাই (Ami dekhi nai) -- I didn't see

আমি খাই নাই (Ami khai nai) -- I didn't eat

Syrian parallel: Negation with নাই (nai) in the past works like ما (ma) in Syrian: যাই নাই = ما رحت. But Bangla puts the negative word AFTER the verb, while Syrian puts ما BEFORE.

5.2 -- Present Perfect ("I have done")

Uses the -ছ- (-chh-) marker:

আমি করেছি (Ami korechhi) -- I have done

আমি খেয়েছি (Ami kheyechhi) -- I have eaten

আমি দেখেছি (Ami dekhechhi) -- I have seen

Note: In everyday Bangladeshi speech, the simple past and present perfect often merge. People commonly say খাই নাই (khai nai) for both "I didn't eat" and "I haven't eaten" -- context makes it clear. Syrian Arabic works the same way (ما أكلت covers both).

5.3 -- Dialogue: Catching Up with a Friend

তানিয়া: আরে! কোথায় ছিলা? কী করলা?
(Tania: Are! Kothay chhila? Ki korla?)
"Hey! Where were you? What did you do?" (like وينك من زمان؟ شو عملتي؟)

সারা: আমি তো ব্যস্ত ছিলাম। গত সপ্তাহে কক্সবাজার গেলাম।
(Sara: Ami to bysto chhilam. Goto shoptahe Cox's Bazar gelam.)

(Tania: Masha'Allah! Amio jete chai.)

✅ Week 6: Future Tense and Making Plans ▶

6.1 -- Simple Future ("I will") and "Let's..."

Bangla future tense uses the -ব- (-b-) marker:

PersonBanglaPronunciationMeaning
আমি (I)করবোkorboI will do
তুমি (informal)করবাkorbayou will do
সে (he/she)করবেkorbehe/she will do
আপনি (formal)করবেনkorbenyou will do
Syrian parallel: In Syrian Arabic, future = رح + verb. In Bangla, future is built into the verb ending. رح روح = যাবো (jabo) / I will go. Simpler -- no separate word needed!

আমি বাজারে যাবো (Ami bazare jabo) -- I will go to the bazaar

কাল কী করবা? (Kal ki korba?) -- What will you do tomorrow?

আমি আসতে পারবো না (Ami ashte parbo na) -- I won't be able to come

"Let's..." -- চলো (cholo):

চলো যাই (Cholo jai) -- Let's go (like يلّا نروح)

চলো খাই (Cholo khai) -- Let's eat (like يلّا ناكل)

চলো বাংলা শিখি (Cholo Bangla shikhi) -- Let's learn Bangla

7.2 -- Yes/No Questions

In Bangla, you can make a yes/no question by adding কি (ki) to a statement, or simply by raising your intonation -- just like Syrian Arabic!

তুমি বাংলাদেশি। (Tumi Bangladeshi.) -- You are Bangladeshi. (statement)

তুমি কি বাংলাদেশি? (Tumi ki Bangladeshi?) -- Are you Bangladeshi? (question)

ভাত খাবা? (Bhat khaba?) -- Will you eat rice? (intonation only)

চা খাবেন? (Cha khaben?) -- Will you have tea? (formal, intonation)
