✅ Grammar Summary -- Negation at a Glance ▶

14.3 -- Negation Reference Table

TensePositiveNegativeExample
Present simpleকরি (kori)করি না (kori na)আমি যাই না (I don't go)
Present continuousকরতেছি (kortechhi)করতেছি না (kortechhi na)আমি খাইতেছি না (I'm not eating)
Simple pastকরলাম (korlam)করি নাই (kori nai)আমি যাই নাই (I didn't go)
Futureকরবো (korbo)করবো না (korbo na)আমি যাবো না (I won't go)
Habitual pastকরতাম (kortam)করতাম না (kortam na)আমি যেতাম না (I wouldn't go)
Imperativeকরো (koro)করো না (koro na)চিন্তা করো না (Don't worry)
Abilityপারি (pari)পারি না (pari na)বলতে পারি না (I can't say)
Key BD marker: নাই (nai) for past/perfect negation (not নই or নি). না (na) comes AFTER the verb in present and future. Note this is opposite to Arabic, where ما comes BEFORE the verb!

Question 1 of 3
Which word is used for past negation in Bangladeshi Bangla?
নাই (nai) is used for past/perfect negation -- a distinctly Bangladeshi marker. না (na) is for present and future.
Question 2 of 3
How does Bangla negation placement differ from Arabic?
Bangla: করি না (verb + na). Arabic: ما بعمل (ma + verb). Opposite positions!
Question 3 of 3
How do you say "I won't go" in Bangla?
আমি যাবো না = "I won't go" -- future যাবো + না. যাই নাই = "I didn't go" (past). যাই না = "I don't go" (present).

✅ Practice Strategy and Final Note ▶

14.4 -- Recommended Practice Strategy

Daily (15 minutes):

  • Practice 5 new words with example sentences
  • Read one dialogue aloud
  • Use one flashcard review session

Weekly (1 hour):

  • Review the week's lesson fully
  • Write a short paragraph about your day in Bangla
  • Have a 15-minute conversation practice with Claude using the week's topic

Monthly:

  • Watch one Bangladeshi নাটক (natok / drama) on YouTube with subtitles
  • Try to follow along with a Bangladeshi YouTuber
  • Write a summary of something you watched in Bangla

Ongoing resources:

  • Bangladeshi dramas (নাটক) on YouTube -- excellent for natural speech and humor
  • Bangla news: Prothom Alo (প্রথম আলো) -- Bangladesh's largest newspaper
  • Music: Bangladeshi artists -- both modern and classic Nazrul/Rabindra sangeet
  • Social media: Follow Bangladeshi pages on Facebook and Instagram to read comments in natural dialect
Final note: Language is alive. This guide gives you the structure, but real fluency comes from speaking with real people, making mistakes, and trying again. Bangladeshis are famously warm and encouraging when foreigners attempt Bangla. As a Syrian Arabic speaker, you have a special advantage: the shared Islamic cultural vocabulary, the love of hospitality, the importance of family, and the warmth of calling strangers "brother" and "auntie" will all feel deeply familiar.
