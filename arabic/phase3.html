9.3 -- Dialogue: Being a Guest

أُمّ أَحْمَد: أَهْلًا وَسَهْلًا! تْفَضَّلِي ٱدْخُلِي. ٱلْبِيت بِيتِك.

(Umm Ahmad: ahlan wa-sahlan! tfaddali udkhli. il-bet betik.) "Welcome! Come in. This house is your house."

سَارَة: شُكْرًا خَالْتُو. ٱللَّه يْخَلِّيكِي.

(Sara: shukran khaltu. alla ykhalliki.) "Thank you, auntie. God bless you."

أُمّ أَحْمَد: تْفَضَّلِي ٱقْعُدِي. رَح جِيبْلِك قَهْوِة.

(Umm Ahmad: tfaddali u'udi. rah jiblak ahwe.) "Please sit. I'll bring you coffee."

سَارَة: لَا تِتْعَبِي حَالِك خَالْتُو!

(Sara: la tit'abi halik khaltu!) "Don't trouble yourself, auntie!" (You MUST say this!)

أُمّ أَحْمَد: أَبَدًا! هَاي قَهْوِة وْبَقْلَاوَة. تْفَضَّلِي.

(Umm Ahmad: abadan! hay ahwe w-ba'lawa. tfaddali.) "Not at all! Here's coffee and baklava."

سَارَة: يِسْلَمُو إِيدِيكِي! كْتِير طَيِّبِة.

(Sara: yislamu ideki! ktir tayyibe.) "Bless your hands! Very delicious."

أُمّ أَحْمَد: صَحْتِين. كُولِي كَمَان!

(Umm Ahmad: sahten. kuli kaman!) "Bon appetit. Eat more!"

Cultural note: أُمّ (Umm = "mother of") + eldest son's name is how married women are commonly addressed. أُمّ أَحْمَد = "Ahmad's mother." Men are called أَبُو (Abu = "father of") + son's name. This kunya is a term of respect and familiarity.

10.4 -- Talking About Family

عِنْدَك إِخْوِة؟ ('indak ikhwe?) -- Do you have siblings?

عِنْدِي أَخ وَاحَد وْأُخْتِين ('indi akh wahad w-ukhten) -- I have one brother and two sisters

أَبُوي شَغَّال بِالتِّجَارِة (abuy shaghghal bi-t-tijara) -- My father works in trade

إِمِّي بْتَطْبُخ أَحْسَن أَكِل (immi btatbukh ahsan akil) -- My mother cooks the best food

خَيِّي ٱلْكْبِير مْتَجَوِّز (khayyi l-kbir mtjawwiz) -- My older brother is married

أُخْتِي بْتِدْرُس بِالْجَامْعَة (ukhti btidrus bi-l-jam'a) -- My sister studies at the university

Possessive suffixes: Family words use possessive suffixes directly -- أَبُوي (abuy, my father), إِمِّي (immi, my mother), أَخُوي (akhuy, my brother), أُخْتِي (ukhti, my sister). These are irregular -- the base word changes slightly when you add the possessive.

Question 1 of 3
How do you say "my mother" using a possessive suffix?
إِمِّي (immi) = my mother. أَبُوي = my father. أُخْتِي = my sister.
Question 2 of 3
What does خَيِّي ٱلْكْبِير مْتَجَوِّز mean?
خَيِّي (khayyi) = my brother, ٱلْكْبِير (l-kbir) = the older, مْتَجَوِّز (mtjawwiz) = married.
Question 3 of 3
What is special about possessive suffixes with family words?
Family words are irregular with possessives -- أَبّ becomes أَبُوي, إِمّ becomes إِمِّي, أَخ becomes أَخُوي.

11.3 -- Exclamations and Reactions

11.4 -- Dialogue: A Rough Day

أَحْمَد: كِيفَك يَا خَيّ؟ شُو مَالَك مْعَصِّب؟

(Ahmad: kifak ya khayy? shu malak m'assib?) "How are you bro? Why are you angry?"

سَامِي: وَٱللَّه تَعْبَان. ٱلْيَوْم كَان كْتِير صَعِب بِالشُّغُل.

(Sami: walla ta'ban. il-yom kan ktir sa'b bi-sh-shughul.) "Honestly I'm tired. Today was very hard at work."

أَحْمَد: يَ حَرَام! شُو صَار؟

(Ahmad: ya haram! shu sar?) "Poor thing! What happened?"

سَامِي: ٱلْمُدِير زَعِلَ عَلَيّ بَدُون سَبَب. وْمَا أَكَلْت شِي كِلّ ٱلنَّهَار. جُوعَان وْزَعْلَان.

(Sami: il-mudir zi'il 'alayy badun sabab. w-ma akalt shi kill in-nahar. ju'an w-za'lan.) "The boss got mad at me for no reason. And I didn't eat all day. Hungry and upset."

أَحْمَد: مَا تْقَلَّق! يَلَّا نْرُوح نَاكُل شَاوَرْمَا. بُكْرَا بْتِنْحَلّ إِنْ شَاءَ ٱللَّه.

(Ahmad: ma t'alla'! yalla nruh nakul shawarma. bukra btinhall insha'alla.) "Don't worry! Let's go eat shawarma. Tomorrow it'll work out, God willing."

14.1 -- Body Language and Gestures

Essential Syrian gestures:
  • No (head tilt): A quick upward tilt of the head + raised eyebrows + optional tongue click ("tsk") = No. This confuses many foreigners who think it means "yes"!
  • Wait (hand): Fingers together, pointing up, moving hand up and down = "Wait" / "Slow down"
  • "What do you want?": Hand out, palm up, fingers together pointing up, bouncing the hand = شُو بِدَّك؟
  • Kissing fingers: Tips of fingers together, kissed, then opened outward = "Delicious!" / "Perfect!"
  • Heart touch: Right hand on chest = Sincerity, gratitude, polite refusal
